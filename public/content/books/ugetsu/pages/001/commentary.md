## The first imbalance

This page follows **鵜月洋’s modern-Japanese translation** of the opening of _The House amid the Reeds_. The tale does not begin with a ghost. It begins with location, continuity, and measure: an old village, a household established since the grandfather’s generation, many fields, and material ease.

The modern translation makes the causal descent especially clear. First comes the inherited order: **祖父の代から**, **田畑もたくさんもって**, **家も豊かに**. Then the sentence turns inward. Katsushirō is **無頓着** and **のんき**; farming appears to him as **煩わしい**. What should preserve the household is felt as an inconvenience.

The final phrase, **貧乏になってしまった**, does more than report poverty. **てしまった** seals the change as completed and quietly regrettable. Before any supernatural presence appears, the house has already begun to lose the form that sustained it.
